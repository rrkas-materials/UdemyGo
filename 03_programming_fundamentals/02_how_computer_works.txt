Circuits and switches-----------------------------------------
    - computers run on electricity
    - electricity can be ON or OFF
coding scheme-------------------------------------------------
    - assign meaning to ON and OFF states
    - ON        1
    - OFF       0
binary digits-------------------------------------------------
    - binary digits : 0, 1
    - 1 digit         2 states        0   1
    - 2 digits        4 states        00  01  10  11
    - 3 digits        8 states        000 001 010 011 100 101 110 111
2^n-----------------------------------------------------------
    - n bits        2^n states
ascii---------------------------------------------------------
    - used to be most popular coding sceme
UTF-8---------------------------------------------------------
    - covers most language of world
    - most popular coding sceme
bits, bytes, kb, mb, gb, tb-----------------------------------
    1 bit               1 binary digit  (0 or 1)
    1 byte              8 bits
    1 kilobyte(kb)      1024 bytes
    1 megabyte(mb)      1024 kb
    1 gigabyte(gb)      1024 mb
    1 terabyte(tb)      1024 gb

    1st generation
        vaccum tubes
        Example:
            ENIAC           [Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer]
            UNIVAC          [Universal Automatic Computer]
    2nd generation
        transistor
        smaller
        Example:
            IBM 1620

    3rd generation
        silicon wafers (Integrated Circuits (ICs))
    4th generation
        Microprocessors
    5th generation
        networking
        ai
